# Lesc
Laboratory of Computer Systems Engineering (LESC)

The **LESC Lab** is a research laboratory dedicated to advancing **Computer Architecture, Reconfigurable Hardware, and Embedded Systems**, with applications spanning diverse domains such as healthcare, agriculture, environmental monitoring, bioinformatics, computer networks, and emerging nanotechnologies.

## Mission

LESC’s mission is to design, develop, and evaluate **high-performance and energy-efficient computing systems**, bridging the gap between advanced hardware architectures and real-world applications. The lab focuses on creating scalable, secure, and domain-specific solutions capable of addressing the growing computational demands of artificial intelligence, machine learning, and large-scale data processing.

## Core Research Areas

### 1. Computer Architecture and Reconfigurable Computing

LESC develops novel architectural models, including:

* **Domain-Specific Architectures (DSAs)** for AI and data-intensive workloads
* **FPGA-based accelerators and overlays**
* **Coarse-Grained Reconfigurable Architectures (CGRAs)**
* **Cloud-based FPGA acceleration frameworks**
* **Heterogeneous CPU-FPGA systems**
* Placement and routing techniques for emerging architectures

The lab has contributed to state-of-the-art solutions in runtime FPGA overlays, graph-based placement and routing, hardware/software co-design, and high-performance accelerators for gene regulatory network simulations and machine learning dataflows.

### 2. Hardware Acceleration for AI and Machine Learning

A strong research line integrates **machine learning with computer architecture**, focusing on:

* Energy-efficient acceleration of AI workloads
* Dataflow optimization for ML models
* Edge-aware graph learning methods
* Federated learning and privacy-preserving AI
* High-performance GPU and FPGA implementations

These efforts support applications ranging from backbone network load prediction to soil quality assessment and UAV navigation.

### 3. Embedded Systems and IoT

LESC designs secure and efficient embedded solutions applied to:

* Internet of Things (IoT) and Internet of Healthcare Things (IoHT)
* Identity management and secure communication
* Visible light communication networks
* Underwater and wireless sensor networks
* Human-body communication systems

The lab combines hardware design, communication protocols, and security mechanisms to build robust distributed systems.

### 4. Security, Privacy, and Blockchain

Research also addresses:

* Secure smart contracts and blockchain systems
* Privacy-preserving healthcare data processing
* Multimodal biometric authentication
* Cryptographic protocols for constrained devices

The integration of hardware acceleration with secure architectures is a distinctive characteristic of LESC’s approach.

### 5. Emerging Nanotechnologies and Design Automation

LESC investigates post-CMOS technologies and nanoarchitectures, including:

* Quantum-dot Cellular Automata (QCA)
* Nanomagnetic Logic (NML)
* Atomic silicon quantum dots
* Automated placement and routing for field-coupled nanocomputing

This research anticipates future computing paradigms beyond traditional silicon scaling.

### 6. Interdisciplinary Applications

The lab applies its architectural and embedded solutions to diverse domains such as:

* Precision agriculture and digital farming
* Environmental monitoring
* Bioinformatics and gene regulatory networks
* Taxonomic and ecological data analysis
* Smart infrastructure and communication systems

### 7. Education in Computer Architecture

LESC is also committed to **innovative engineering education**, developing:

* Interactive RISC-V teaching tools
* Online learning modules using Google Colab and Gradio
* AIoT-based educational platforms
* Practical hardware/software co-design tutorials

## Vision

LESC Lab envisions a future where **reconfigurable, energy-efficient, and application-aware computing architectures** enable sustainable and secure digital transformation across industries and scientific fields.

By combining **computer architecture expertise with interdisciplinary collaboration**, LESC positions itself at the intersection of hardware innovation and impactful real-world applications.
